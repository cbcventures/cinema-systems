SETTING UP YOUR CUSTOM HOME THEATER SYSTEM FOR COMFORT

1/15/2019

0 Comments

 
SETTING UP YOUR CUSTOM HOME THEATER SYSTEM FOR COMFORTWith many things to consider when designing your custom home theater, things can get very complicated along the way. There are so many things to consider when designing your theater space. However, most people do not have the space or money for a proper design.  However, you shouldn’t worry about that.
Custom home theater systems are mostly placed in small bedrooms.  Sometimes (if you have the extra space), it can be placed in the living room or office, meant. Designing a home theater in a normal room where all the elements can not be controlled, can be difficult.  It could result to some mistakes.
DON’T LOOK UPPeople are not created to look up. Even a little upward look for two or three hours can cause severe pains to the shoulders and the neck. Most ergonomists recommend positioning the screen at the center of one’s view to avoid any pain.
This implies that when mounting the television or monitor, it should always be placed at the center of your view.  Ideally between 4 and 5 feet above the floor.  But it depends on how high or low the seats are in the custom home theater.
DON’T SIT TOO FAR OR CLOSEHow far or close you sit to your custom home theater system matters a lot. Sitting too close can be very bad, and your eyes could get affected.  However, nobody wants that. If you have a poor eye sight, then you should not be too close to the screen as this can only make it worse. On the other hand, sitting too far is also bad.  Since this can make you not to appreciate the view.
DON’T ALLOW THE SUN TO RUIN YOUR THEATERLighting has a great impact on the image quality, so this should be one of your top priorities. Try as much as you can not to place your screen around the sun. Don’t place it anywhere near direct sunlight when you are viewing it. Make every effort to place your television in an optimal spot. Never place it where direct sunlight will flash on the screen while you’re watching it.  Also, don’t place it opposite a big window.
If sunlight penetrate into your rooms, consider buying blinds or blackout shades. These will help to keep the lights out of the room on a hot sunny day.

PREVENT YOUR SMART HOME FROM BEING HACKED

11/3/2018

0 Comments

 
HOW TO PREVENT YOUR SMART HOME FROM BEING HACKED?We are in the era of smart technology.  It is least likely to find anything today that does not involve smart devices.  Starting from smartphones to smartwatches and Home Automation systems, we are literally surrounded. Although home automation has made our life easier, it comes with danger. Sure, we are able to perform almost all the tasks like controlling lighting, climate, entertainment systems and appliances. However, that does also put the privacy of your personal life and security at stake.  Potentially, it provides access to home automation system to hackers.
Now here are some typical threats and ways to tackle these threats to make your home automation safer.
THREATSThe biggest security threat to home automation is an unsafe router. You see router is the pivot point of the whole system. If a person is able to access the router that actually means your whole home automation system is toast. So, you need to secure your router with a strong password.  Also, do not forget to change the default password of your router. A safe router is actually the basic line of defense against all the cyber threats.
Secondly, most of the ISPs provide you with the facility to check the history of which devices have accessed your router. So, you might consider reviewing the history or logs frequently.
Moreover, almost all the smart devices in the world are vulnerable to hacking. So, with every smart device in your home automation system, the security of your system is compromised. However, this threat of security can be lowered by using smart devices built by reputable brands. Although not all the companies consider the security of their products seriously, some do. So smart home installation requires you to exercise due care in choosing the brand for your appliances.
COMPANIESThe companies that provide all such appliances for your home automation system also test fixes to bugs.  As result, they do offer an updates in the software of the device. So, make sure that your devices are updated according to the latest software to avoid any security threat.
WI-FI SECURITYThere is also a threat to your private information while you are using public Wi-Fi because a public Wi-Fi is more open to hackers. So, make sure not to operate any home automation application on any public Wi-Fi . Also, consider to frequently changing the passwords of the connected devices to your system (including the router).  And always keep strong ones, this makes the security of your system strong.
HIRE SOMEONEYou may also prefer to hire a network security consultant who can guide you to make your network more secure. Also, it is always advisable to have a proper home automation security system with layers.  For example,a  fingerprint sensor to add another layer of protection.
So, by following all these measures, you can make your home automation system safer and less exposed to threats. With just a little care, you can keep enjoying the benefits of a home automation system without the fears of any hack into the system.
